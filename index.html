Remove a Block
See the full impact of removing a block — variable costs that stop, fixed costs that get reallocated to remaining blocks, and the net change to whole-farm profitability.
Key concept: When you remove a block, its variable costs stop. But the farm's fixed costs (equipment, management, insurance) don't disappear — they get spread over fewer acres, raising per-acre overhead for every remaining block. Sometimes keeping a marginal block is better than removing it.
